reaction rules

desc
Ligand binding (L+R)
note
specifying r in R here means that the r component must not be bound. This prevents dissociation of ligand from R when R is in a dimer.
source
L(r) + R(l,r) <-> L(r!1).R(l!1,r) kpL, kmL
desc
Aggregation (R+R)
note
R must be bound to ligand to dimerize.
source
L(r!1).R(l!1,r) + L(r!1).R(l!1,r) <-> L(r!1).R(l!1,r!3).L(r!2).R(l!2,r!3) kpD,kmD
desc
Receptor binding to adaptor (R+A)
note
A and R can bind independent of whether A is bound to K or whether R is in a dimer.
source
A(r) + R(a) <-> A(r!1).R(a!1) kpA,kmA
desc
Adaptor binding kinase
note
Doesn't depend on phosphorylation state of K or whether A is bound to receptor, i.e. binding rate is same whether A is on membrane (bound to R) or in cytosol.
source
A(k) + K(a) <-> A(k!1).K(a!1) kpK,kmK
desc
Kinase transphosphorylation by inactive kinase
note
Rule doesn't specify how two K's are associated
source
K(Y~U).K(Y~U) -> K(Y~U).K(Y~P) pK
desc
Kinase transphosphorylation by active kinase
note
Rule doesn't specify how two K's are associated
source
K(Y~P).K(Y~U) -> K(Y~P).K(Y~P) pKs
desc
Dephosphorylation of kinase in cytosol
source
K(a,Y~P) -> K(a,Y~U) dC
